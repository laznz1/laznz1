MIT Student Council Meeting and Updates

by Laurence Earl August 20, 2017
Hi MIT,
Student Council met on the 9th of August to discuss upcoming events and plans here is an outline of things discussed

Sugarfree at MIT
After a lot of student consultation and discussion, The Student Council has decided to provisionally take the first steps into creating a sugar-free environment at MIT.
There is a lot more information over at the Sugarfree Project page

O-Week Success
Mel Ngata and her team worked hard to help provide a fun and enjoyable O-Week for everyone!

The awesome photos are available at the Student Council Facebook page!

Nursing Move to MIT Manukau in 2018
It was recently announced that the Faculty of Nursing will be moving and joining the Faculty of Bussiness and IT at the Manukau Campus in 2018. This is an awesome and positive move for Nursing as building and space concerns were becoming an increasing issue.
Students at Manukau are being asked to have their say about how the campus can be restructured to make room for the nursing students. There was an open forum held on Wednesday the 16th of August to discuss this further and to get your views and opinions about this move.
Please contact us directly using the contact us form or make sure you fill out your digital diaries!

MIT Open Forum
MIT Manukau had their first Open Forum on Wednesday the 16th August.
Thank you very much for coming more update about this soon.

Carparking at MIT Manukau
I met with Steve Simpson (Head of Property and Campus Services) and John Wadsworth (Dean of FOBIT) on Tuesday to discuss the issues with Car parking availability and condition of the Carpark at MIT Manukau. Steve will be running an investigation into who utilises the Car park to get some valuable data. Steve is committed to improving and alleviating issues with the Carpark

MIT Student Ball/Social
After the votes at O-Week were counted it seems clear that MIT Student would like a Social events MIT Student Council have began discussion with Student Support and Student Executive Voice

Maori Representive on the Student Voice
Tena koutou katoa. We are seeking a Maori Representative for the Student Executive Voice.If you know of someone who is registered as a Maori tauira and would like torepresent Maori tauira at MIT, please send their name, Student ID, contact email and mobile, and a short statement about them by 23rd August 12pm to studentvoice@Manukau.ac.nz

Enrol to Vote!
With Elections 2017 right around the corner, It's now more important than ever to make sure you and everyone around you are enrolled to vote! Only 64% of people aged 18-24 are enrolled to vote! Pass this information on to your classmates and teams!
Enrol to Vote Here!

Student Council Meeting July

Meeting Updates by Laurence Earl July 13, 2017
Hi MIT, Today we had the monthly Student Council meeting here is a quick run down on topics discussed
Sugarfree at MIT
Is a project which Diane King and her team presented to us. The initiative would remove sugared and sweetened drinks from being sold at MIT in the Vending machines and the Canteens. The Student Council want your opinion on this before we make a decision if we would like to support this.
